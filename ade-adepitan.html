Who is Ade Adepitan? 

Ade Adepitan is a Paralympic star who won gold medals in wheelchair basketball, wheelchair tennis, and dance. He uses a wheelchair as a reason to contract polio as a kid that caused his left leg uses. Adepitan is currently a BBC and Channel 4 employer. He also worked as an actor and currently working as a British Television Presenter. 

Adedoyin Olayiwola "Ade" Adepitan was born 27 March 1973 in Maryland, Lagos, Nigeria. Adepitan contracted polio at the age of fifteen months, resulting in the loss of function of his left leg and eventually preventing him from walking. 

Adepitan and his mom immigrated to the United Kingdom at the age of three to join his dad who resided in Newham's London Borough. Then, he started to study at Plaistow's Southern Road Primary School, which he credits to help him with his handicap and home issues. 

Adepitan is an accomplished wheelchair basketball player for his club Milton Keynes Aces and as a member of the UK group. The group won the bronze medal at the 2004 Athens Summer Paralympics and the gold medal at the 2005 Manchester Paralympic World Cup. As for now, Adepitan is working as a British television presenter in BBC News. Lerato Mbele, Katty Kay, and Carol Kirkwood are also part of BBC News along with Ade.
