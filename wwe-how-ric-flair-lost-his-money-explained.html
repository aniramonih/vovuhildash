Ric Flair Got In Trouble With The IRS

Flair Feuded With THe IRS (& Not Mike Rotunda)

Close

  • Flair invested his money in the incorrect places, causing issues

Throughout the 1980s, Flair did not pay taxes, making the IRS his rival for the rest of his life. The IRS came knocking on his door in the 1990s, and while Flair was able to pay the owed amount, this set the tone for the rest of his life. In 2005, the IRS seized his earnings to pay back the taxes he owed. As recently as 2019, Flair was served a notice to pay $280,000 and the troubles continue to this day. Flair was also prone to investing in the wrong places. The Ric Flair Finance program was struck down almost immediately as it was illegal. Flair had invested quite a lot of his money into the project, albeit he was pushed into the project by a shady guy named Chris Porter. Porter then sued Flair for a loan he was owed and that brings up factor number three; Flair took a lot of loans and barely paid them back.

Ric Flair Was Helped Out By Vince McMahon

Billionaire Vince McMahon Got Ric Flair Out Of Trouble

Close
  • Vince McMahon himself has shelled out a lot of money throughout his life
  • McMahon got Ric Flair out of some tough spots
  • McMahon has a close bond to those who have been loyal to him

As previously noted, Ric Flair ended up both spending and borrowing a lot of money, and he revealed a lot of this information himself, being very open about his financial troubles. Speaking on a WWE Network edition of the Broken Skull Sessions podcast with Steve Austin, Flair revealed that Vince McMahon said to him after lending him a huge lump of money.

"When I've had financial woes, through my divorces, he's lent me money. But I've paid him back. One time, I was into him for $800,000. Eight hundred grand. He (Vince) said, 'I wouldn't lend my kid this kind of money.'"

There are many wrestlers who have a strong, close bond to Vince McMahon, and this is something that certainly helped Flair out of a sticky situation. Not everything was doom and gloom for Flair, noting in that interview with Austin that;

"I've been rich, I've been poor, I've owed the IRS $1 million once, $2 million once. I'm even, no bankruptcy, and I can probably count a million dollars that I've got right now. And everything is paid for."
