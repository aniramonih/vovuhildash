Richard Samuel Morris is an American political writer and commentator, who previously worked as a pollster, political campaign consultant, and general policy consultant. As a friend and advisor to Bill Clinton during his tenure as governor of Arkansas, since his election in 1978, Morris became a political advisor to the White House after Clinton was elected president in 1992. Morris encouraged Clinton to pursue a traditional combination triangle policy. Proposals, rhetoric and questions from Republicans and Democrats for maximum political gain and popularity. He was a Republican strategist before joining the Clinton administration, where he helped to recover Clinton from the 1994 midterm elections by advising the president to adopt more dovish policies. The President consulted Morris in secret from 1994 onwards .
