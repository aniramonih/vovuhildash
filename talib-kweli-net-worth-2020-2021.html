Career

It was in the year 1996 when Talib Kweli made his rapping debut. He showcased his talent in an album known as Doom by Cincinnati and Ohio Group Mood. It was in Cincinnati that Talib met with DJ Hi-Tek. In the later years, these two collaborated on specific projects like Reflection Eternal. During the period between 1998 and 2000, Talib returned to New York. He formed a group named Black Star with Mos Def. They released an album known as Mos Def and Talib Kweli are the black star, and Hi-Tek produced it. This album was released in 1998.

In 2000 Talib along with Hi-Tek made an album called, Train of thought. In the year 2000, both Mos Def and Talib Kweli worked on a project called the Hip Hop for Respect. This was mainly organized for protesting against the Amadou Diallo case. In the year 2001, Talib and Mos made their contribution to the compilation album known as Red Hot + Indigo. It was in 2002 when Kweli released his solo album. He also a part of the album, The College Dropout which was Kanye West’s debut album that achieved huge success. Talib has worked with Rawkus records and also Blacksmith records, and in the year 2011, he founded his record label known as Javotti Media. Talib Kweli is also known for his activist, social works.

Awards and achievements

In the year 2014, Talib Kweli was nominated for the BET hip-hop awards for his song State of Grace. Then in 2007, he was nominated for MTVU woodie awards. In 2011 and 2013 he got a nomination for O music awards and Soul train awards respectively.
