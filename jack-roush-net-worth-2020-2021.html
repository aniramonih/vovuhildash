Early Life & Biography

Roush was born and raised for a few years in the Covington city of Kentucky state, United States. He later moved to Manchester, Ohio, where he spent the rest of his adolescence. He graduated from Berea College, Kentucky, with a bachelor’s degree in physics and minor mathematics. Jack got his master’s degree in scientific mathematics from Eastern Michigan University, Michigan. In 1966, he joined Ford and left in 1970 to start his own business.  He also worked at Chrysler, one of the three big automobile manufacturers in the United States, while to grow his own startup.

Career

Roush had a very interesting career life. After leaving Ford, determined to start his own business, he worked in Chrysler for a small while. He finally managed to open his own engineering firm, and it all started from there. He teamed up with Wayne Gapp (a racing car driver who was one half of the Gapp & Roush drag racing team) and raced in NHRA, IHRA and AHRA drag racing events.

His firm also partnered up with a German company to make racing cars for Ford, which lead to the rise of the IMSA Camel GT championship and Trans-Am series of cars. His teams have won a total of seven championships. In addition to racing, his company Roush Enterprise owns Roush Performance, which designs and sells parts of cars (this also leads to another famous Ford series, the Mustangs). The enterprise also owns ROUSH CleanTech and Roush Industries.

The entrepreneur has been involved in several plane crashes that risked his life. On his 60th birthday in 2002, Roush had a near-death experience when his private plane Aircam landed in Troy, Alabama. Eight years later, he had another plane crash in Livonia, where he almost lost one of his eyes.
