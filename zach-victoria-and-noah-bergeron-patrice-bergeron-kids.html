Who Are Zach, Victoria &amp; Noah Bergeron? Patrice Bergeron Kids

NHL franchise icon Patrice Bergeron has three kids; a daughter and two sons.

Patrice Bergeron, born on July 24, 1985, is a Canadian professional ice hockey center and captain of the Boston Bruins of the National Hockey League (NHL).

The 38-year-old veteran started his ice hockey career with the Acadie–Bathurst Titan of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League in 1998.

After playing an entire season with the minor ice hockey team, the Bruins selected him as the 45th overall pick in the 2003 NHL Entry Draft.

Since joining Bruins, Bergeron has become a legend in the franchise. He has featured in three Stanley Cups and won one in 2011 against the Vancouver Canucks, where he scored two goals.

He is ranked fourth on the franchise’s all-time scoring list and is third all-time in games played.

Bergeron has won five Frank J. Selke Trophy, the most by any player in NHL history, awarded annually to the NHL forward with the best defensive skills.

Moreover, the right-handed center has won five gold medals with the Canadian national team at the 2004 World Championships, 2005 World Junior Championships, 2010 Winter Olympics, 2012 Spengler Cup, and 2014 Winter Olympics.
