Bryce Mitchell Bio, Age, Parents, Wife, Children, Net Worth

Bryce Mitchell Bio, Age, Parents, Wife, Children, Net Worth – Bryce Mitchell is a professional MMA fighter from the United States. He currently competes in the Ultimate Fighting Championship’s Featherweight division (UFC).

Mitchell made an appearance on the reality show The Ultimate Fighter: Undefeated. He is currently ranked #9 in the UFC featherweight rankings as of March 7, 2022.

Bryce Mitchell Biography

Bryce Mitchell grew up in Cabot, Arkansas, and attended Cabot High School. He participated in a variety of sports, including varsity basketball and wrestling, where he finished second in the 7A state championships as a junior and fourth in the 6A state championships as a senior. Mitchell moved to Searcy, Arkansas, after high school to attend Harding University.

Mitchell earned a bachelor’s degree in economics from Harding University. He is a cattle farmer in addition to his MMA career.

Mitchell is a political outspoken man who believes that the US government created SARS-CoV-2 in a lab and deliberately released it, and that the US government stages mass shootings to advance a gun control agenda.

He has also called the Federal Reserve a “corrupt institution” that manipulates the value of the United States dollar. Mitchell is also a flat Earther, geocentrist, and believes gravity is a hoax.

Mitchell was chosen for The Ultimate Fighter: Undefeated Featherweight tournament after compiling a 9-0 professional MMA record. He defeated Jay Cucciniello in the quarterfinals but lost in the semi-finals to eventual season winner Brad Katona. Mitchell was signed by the UFC despite the loss.

Mitchell faced Tyler Diamond on July 6, 2018 at The Ultimate Fighter: Undefeated. He was victorious by unanimous decision.

Mitchell faced Bobby Moffett on March 23, 2019 at UFC Fight Night: Thompson vs. Pettis. He was declared the winner by a unanimous vote. This match was dubbed “Fight of the Night.”

Mitchell fought Matt Sayles at UFC on ESPN: Overeem vs. Rozenstruik on December 7, 2019. He won the fight via twister submission in the first round, which was only the second twister submission victory in UFC history.

This victory earned him the Performance of the Night award, as well as the 2019 Submission of the Year title from sites such as Sherdog, MMAJunkie, and MMA Fighting. Mitchell then signed a new four-fight contract with the UFC.

Mitchell was set to face Charles Rosa at UFC Fight Night: Hermansson vs. Weidman on May 2, 2020. However, on April 9, Dana White, the president of the UFC, announced that the event had been postponed and that the fight would now take place on May 9, 2020 at UFC 249.
