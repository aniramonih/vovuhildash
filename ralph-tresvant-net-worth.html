Ralph Tresvant is an American singer, record producer, and actor who has a net worth of $8 million. Tresvant debuted as a solo artist with the album Ralph Tresvant in 1990 which sold two million copies, achieving double-platinum certification.

Ralph is best known as the lead singer of the New Edition R&B group. In 2008, he formed a new band, Heads of State, with Bobby Brown and Johnny Gill. Tresvant’s vocals have also been featured on the soundtracks of several films.

Ralph Edward Tresvant was born in the Roxbury neighborhood of Boston, Massachusetts, on May 16, 1968, to Patricia Tresvant and Ralph Hall. LaTonya and Andre are Trasvent’s two siblings.

They signed with MCA Records. His self-titled debut album went double platinum after selling two million copies. The song titled Sensitivity, the lead single, lasted 20 weeks on the R&B Singles chart in the United States, including two weeks at the top.

The Top 5 hit singles were also included on his album. Tresvant’s vocals also appeared on the soundtracks for the films Mo’ Money and The Preacher’s Wife, and he made a cameo appearance in the film House Party 2.

It featured his two singles for the soundtrack. As a solo artist, he released two more albums in addition to his self-titled album in 1990, titled It’s Goin’ Down in 1994 and Rizz-Wa-Faire in 2006.

He has also appeared solo in the movies Barbershop Blues, Triple Cross, Behind RizzWaFaire, and Get on Up as well as with New Edition in Knight Rider, Krush Groove, and Girls Trip.

In 2008, he started touring and performing with the new band Heads of State alongside Johnny Gill and Bobby Brown. He is also a radio show DJ on Boston’s WZBR 1410 AM The BASS, and his show “Inside the Ride with Ralph Tresvant” in 2016.
