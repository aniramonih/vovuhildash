Sherry Shriner- Her Life, Death, Conspiracies, Controversies, Other Quick Facts

Sherry Shriner was an American author, blogger, preacher, and leader of the Alien Reptile Cult. She gave herself the status of being the messenger of the “Most High God”. She reached out to followers in her writings and teachings which centered around the fight against the deceiver of mankind, the devil.

From an early part of her life, Sherry was different from others of her generation. She yielded so much influence that cost many people their lives while weaving different conspiracies.

Table of Contents

Who is Sherry Shriner?

She was born in 1965 in Cleveland, Ohio. The reptile alien cult boss attended both high school and college. She earned a degree in journalism, political science, and criminal justice from Kent State University.

Her unusual attack on the devil attracted a lot of attention to her course. The relationship between Sherry and the devil started when she was a kid. While other kids were distracted with toys and the likes, Sherry had started talking with the Devil. She changed her life cause from serving him to fighting it.

Who Was Sherry Shriner’s Husband and How Did She Die?

Her life was surrounded by many conspiracy theories and controversies that have made her family stay in the shadows. Sherry was married and had four children who reside in Carrollton, Ohio. The identity of members of her family is not known at the time of this writing.

She died on January 28 2021 as a result of a heart attack at the age of 56. Even in death, Shriner still has a large following that still attaches itself to her teachings.

She Allegedly Had Dined With The Devil Amidst Other Controversies

There are many controversies surrounding the work of Sherry Shriner. Her book publication was shocking to many because of its controversial nature. The book revealed the conversations she had with the devil whom she referred to as Lucifer.

She claimed to have once been in heaven with Lucifer before she turned against him when she realized his deception. This however is not the only controversy about the woman. Here are others.

Sherry Shriner accused NATO of the death of her members

The cult she leads had its share of tragedies. There were two cases of suicide linking it to the cult.  In Sherry’s defense, she claimed that the members were killed by shadowy figures and also linked it to the North Atlantic Treaty Organization (NATO). After one of her members, Kelly Pingilley’s body was found in the woods, many had their suspicion of the type of organization she led.

She was accused of leading the murder of her member, Steve Mineo

Sherry’s name became popular after the death of one of the Alien Reptile’s strong members, Steve Mineo. Steve and his girlfriend, Barbara Rogers were active members till Barbara’s Facebook update on eating raw steak led to a misunderstanding with Sherry. He died on July 15 2017 after Barbara shot him in the head.

Barbara called the police after the shooting and told them he had asked her to do so. She also claimed she shot him under the influence of Shriner. Steve was 32 at the time of his death and had been having problems with the cult after he tried to expose it.

Rogers was later sentenced to 15 to 40 years in prison but she maintained that she was not the one in control the day Steve died.

Did You know These Facts About Sherry Shriner?

1. She was once a practicing Lutheran

Although it is difficult to trace her earliest practices, sources claim that at a point in her life, Sherry was a practicing Lutheran before she shifted sides.

2. She started a cult against the New World Order

Her claim on a huge following of believers was indoctrinated against the New world order. She held the belief that there was a New World Order led by shape-shifting politicians. She accused everyone from Queen Elizabeth to Barack Obama as part of those that will dominate the earth and wanted to establish a one-world government.

3. Sherry Shriner did seminars and lectures to send out her message

Ever since she was said to turn against Lucifer, she started to be vocal about her fight with the devil that she taught others. Sherry organizes seminars and lectures to enlighten people.

She started using social media to do most of her teachings, especially Facebook where she had large followership. Her Monday nights blog radio talk show ran on a weekly basis and was greatly influenced by her late follower, kelly Pingilley. Sherry launched many websites like TheWatcherFiles.com, SherryShriner.com, and OrgoneBlaster.com to reach many members.

4. Orgone was central to her “Supernatural Teachings”

Shriner advises her followers to have orgone, a supernatural substance. Orgone allegedly had the powers to defeat clones and synthetic robots used to install the new world order.

Many of her followers owned necklaces or other forms of the substance. Pingelley’s body was found with an orgone necklace at the park where she killed herself.
