Age, Biography and Wiki

Neil Cavuto is an American television presenter and business journalist. He is the senior vice president of Fox News and the host of Your World with Neil Cavuto and Cavuto Live on Fox News Channel. He is also the host of Cavuto: Coast to Coast on Fox Business Network. Cavuto was born on September 22, 1958 in Westbury, New York. He graduated from St. Bonaventure University in 1980 with a degree in mass communication. Cavuto began his career in journalism in 1980 as a reporter for WXIX-TV in Cincinnati, Ohio. He then moved to WBZ-TV in Boston, Massachusetts, where he worked as a reporter and anchor. In 1984, he joined CNBC as a reporter and anchor. In 1996, Cavuto joined Fox News Channel as its business news anchor and managing editor. He has since become one of the most recognizable faces on the network. Cavuto has won numerous awards for his work, including the National Headliner Award, the New York Festivals Award, and the Edward R. Murrow Award. Timeline

As reported June 22, 2016, by Charles Payne on Your World, Cavuto has undergone, and is recovering from, recent cardiac surgery.

Cavuto joined Fox News in July 1996, and became host and managing editor of Your World with Neil Cavuto when the network launched in October. He became a vice president of business news in March 2006. He serves all three positions concurrently. Your World is Fox's main business news program.

Before joining Fox, Cavuto was a principal anchor and reporter for CNBC. He co-anchored CNBC's initial broadcast on April 17, 1989. Cavuto hosted Power Lunch on CNBC and contributed to NBC's Today. He worked with the Public Broadcasting Service for 15 years reporting for The Nightly Business Report. He was also a New York City bureau chief.

Cavuto and his wife, Mary Fulling, whom he married on October 15, 1983, have three children: Tara, Bradley and Jeremy. They reside in Mendham, New Jersey.

Cavuto was born in Westbury, New York, on Long Island, as the son of Kathleen T. (Feeley), a United Nations staffer and housewife, and Patrick "Pat" Cavuto, a can company sales executive. His father was of Italian descent, while his mother was of Irish ancestry. He was raised in Danbury, Connecticut, where he attended Immaculate High School. At 17, he became the manager of a fish and chips restaurant while attending high school. He worked as a White House intern during U.S. president Jimmy Carter's administration. He graduated from St. Bonaventure University in 1980 with a bachelor's degree in mass communication, and earned a master's degree from American University.

Neil Cavuto (born September 22, 1958) is an American television news anchor, commentator, and business journalist for Fox News. He hosts three television programs: Your World with Neil Cavuto and Cavuto Live, both on Fox News, and Cavuto: Coast to Coast on sister channel Fox Business Network since January 20, 2018.
