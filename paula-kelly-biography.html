Who is Paula Kelly?

Paula Kelly is an American film actress, singer, dancer, and theatre actress, who made her Broadway debut as Mrs. Veloz in the 1964 musical ‘Something More!’ alongside Barbara Cook. Her other appearances include in ‘The Dozens’ in 1969, and ‘Sophisticated ladies’ in 1981.

In which school did Paula Kelly attend?

Paula Kelly attended the Fiorello H. LaGuardia High School of Music and Art and majored in music. Later, she continued her studies at the Julliard School of Music and majored in dance under Martha Hill. Also, she graduated with an  M.S. degree and later performed as a soloist with major modern dance companies such as Donald McKayle, Alvin Ailey, and Martha Graham.

Paula Kelly: Professional Life and Career

She portrayed the guest role in the television musicals ‘Sammy and Friends and ‘Peter Pan’ in which she appeared as Tiger-Lily. Then, she appeared in ‘The Richard Pryor Show and Gene Kelly’s ‘New York, New York.

Later, Paula Kelly appeared on the London stage in ‘Sweet Charity’ alongside actress and dancer Juliet Prowse. She made her appearance in ‘Don’t Bother Me, ‘I Can’t Cope’, etc.

Her other notable film appearances include ‘Top of the Heap’, ‘The Spook Who Sat by the Door’, ‘Jo Jo Dancer, Your Life is calling’, ‘Bank Robber’, ‘Once Upon a Time… When We Were Colored’, and many more.

Paula Kelly: Awards, Nominations

Paula Kelly was nominated for Primetime Emmy Award in the category Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Special for ‘The Women of Brewster Place’ in 1989, nominated for the ACE Award in the category Supporting Actress in a Movie or Miniseries for ‘Uncle Tom’s Cabin’ in 1998.
