Singer’s Bio and Age

Josey Scott was born on May 3, 1972, in Memphis, Tennessee. He grew up in a musical family, where he learned to play the guitar and sing at a young age. Career

Scott’s career started when he formed his first band, “Snot,” in 1995. The band quickly gained popularity, and their debut album, “Get Some,” became a massive hit. Thanks to their raw energy and hard-hitting lyrics, they managed to create a loyal fan base that followed them wherever they went. However, tragedy struck the band when their lead singer, Lynn Strait, died in a car accident in 1998.

This tragedy did not stop Scott from pursuing his passion for music. Together with other band members, he formed a new group called “The Union Underground.” This band had a different sound and style compared to “Snot.” They released two studio albums, “An Education in Rebellion” in 2001 and “Live… One Nation Underground” in 2002. However, “The Union Underground” disbanded in 2005.

Scott then decided to focus on his solo career. He released his first album, “Sinister,” in 2011 and another album, “The Tyrants and Kings” in 2012, which showcased his growth as an artist. In 2014, he joined the band “Rock-N-Roll Soldiers” as their lead vocalist and released an album called “So Many Musicians To Kill.”
