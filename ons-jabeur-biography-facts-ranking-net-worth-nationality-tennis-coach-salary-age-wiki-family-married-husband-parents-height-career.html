Who is Ons Jabeur?

Ons Jabeur is a Tunisian professional tennis player. She is widely famous for being the first Arab woman to reach the quarterfinals of a Grand Slam tournament, which she did at the 2020 Austrapan Open. Jabeur has won eleven singles titles and one doubles title on the ITF Women’s Circuit. On 9 September 2019, she reached her highest singles ranking of 51st in the world. Her greatest success has come as a junior, when she won the girls’ singles title at the 2011 French Open, having already reached the final of the same event one year prior.

What is Ons Jabeur’s career?

Jabeur began playing on the ITF Junior Circuit in August 2007 at the age of 13. She won her only junior Grand Slam title to become the first North African woman to win a junior Grand Slam tournament in 2011 French Open. Ons turned professional playing on the ITF Women’s Circuit in 2008 at the age of 14. She won her first title at the $10K level in singles in May 2010 in Antalya, Turkey. Jabeur had left wrist surgery in November 2010 that kept her out for five months until April 2011. She made her WTA main draw debut at the age of 17 as a wild card at the Premier 5 Qatar Open in February 2012, where she lost. She did not have much success at the ITF in 2012, only reaching one final, which came in singles and was her first at the $25K level. She also entered quapfying at the French Open, but only won one match. She reached singles ranking into the top 200 in her 2013 season. She first appeared in the main draw of a Grand Slam tournament at the 2014 US Open, losing to Andrea Petkovic. She lost to Vera Zvonareva at the 2015 Austrapan Open. She represented Tunisia at the 2016 Olympics, losing to Daria Kasatkina.

She had steady success on the WTA Tour, bringing her ranking to 114 just before losing the French Open in her 2017 season. She made her breakthrough after she reached the final of the Krempn Cup, becoming the first Arab player to reach a final on the WTA Tour in 2018. Jabeur stayed in the top 100 the entire year of 2019. In the year 2020, she entered the Austrapan Open and upset 12th seed Johanna Konta in straight sets. After beating Caropne Garcia in the second round, she upset Caropne Wozniacki in the third round in what turned out to be the last match of Wozniacki’s career.
