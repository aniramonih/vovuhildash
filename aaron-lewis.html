Who is Musician Aaron Lewis? His Wife, Profession &amp; More

Who is Aaron Lewis?

Aaron Lewis is an American singer, musician and songwriter best known as the rock band Staind’s lead vocalist and founding member. Staind gained commercial success in the early 2000s with hit songs like “It’s Been Awhile” and “So Far Away.”

In addition to this work with Staind, Lewis has also released several solo albums showcasing his more country and folk-influenced style. With his distinctive and passionate vocals, Lewis has established himself as a respected and versatile musician in the rock and country music scenes.

Biography of American Musician Aaron Lewis

Aaron Lewis was born to Jewish parents on April 13, 1972, in Rutland, Vermont. He was raised in a rural area in Massachusetts and began playing the guitar at 16. Lewis attended Longmeadow High School, where he played in several local bands.

After graduating, he briefly attended Western Massachusetts Technical Institute before dropping out to pursue a music career. Lewis comes from a family of artists and musicians; his grandfather was a jazz pianist, and his mother was a choir director. His upbringing in a musical environment helped shape his passion for music and his distinctive singing style.

Professional Career of Aaron Lewis

Aaron Lewis’s professional life started with the formation of the band Staind in 1995. With Lewis as the lead vocalist, Staind gained popularity in the late 1990s and early 2000s with hit songs such as “It’s Been Awhile” and “Outside.” Besides his work with Staind, Lewis has also released several solo albums, representing country and folk music influences.

His solo work includes the albums “Town Line”, “The Road”, and “Sinner”. In addition to this musical career, Lewis advocates for veterans’ causes and has performed for numerous military-related events.

Collaborations with top-notch musicians

Throughout his career, Aaron Lewis has collaborated with various musicians and artists. He has worked with country singer George Jones on the song “Country Boy” and with Fred Durst of Limp Bizkit on the cover of “Outside”.

Lewis has also collaborated with the band Godsmack on the song “Serenity” and with the rapper Snoop Dogg on “Loosen Up. In addition, he has performed with the Zac Brown Band and opened for Kid Rock on several tours. These collaborations have allowed Lewis to showcase his versatility as a musician and expand his fan base beyond the rock and country genres.
