Early Life

Christopher Allen Lloyd was born on the 22nd of October of the year 1938. The 79 years old actor hails from Stamford, Connecticut. His father was an attorney whereas his mother was a singer. He grew up four sisters and two brothers.

He went through 4 marriages out of which every single one ended up in a divorce.

The Texaco Oil company was co-founded by Christopher Lloyd’s grandfather. He attended the Darrow School, Fessenden School, and the Neighbourhood Playhouse School of the Theatre.

He pursued his interest in acting since a very early age. When he was 14 years old, he worked as an apprentice in summer stock. At the Neighbourhood Playhouse School of the Theatre, he was taking classes by Sanford Meisner. He was 19 years old at that time.

And They Put Handcuffs on the Flowers marked itself as Christopher Lloyd’s debut on the stage. It was a production by Fernando Arrabal. Red, White and Maddox marked itself as Lloyd’s Broadway debut. Even though it didn’t garner a huge audience, it was Lloyd’s first close encounter with the delicacies of acting.

Career

Before moving to film, Christopher Lloyd worked with as many as 200 stage productions. His first role on the big screen came in the film, ‘One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest’. He acted as a psychiatric patient named Max Taber.

Owing to the film’s enormous success, Lloyd went to California to pursue a proper career in film. His next big role was that of Dr Emmett “Doc” Brown in the Back to the Future Series starring Michael J. Fox. The first Back to the Future movie came in the year 1985, followed by the second and the third part in the years 1989 and 1990 respectively.

His role as Dr Emmett “Doc” Brown is till date considered to be his most acclaimed one. He also starred in the Star Trek franchise. He assumed the role of Klingon in ‘Star Trek III: The Search for Spock’. Some of the other popular movies that Christopher Lloyd starred in include ‘Clue’, ‘The Addams Family’, ‘Addams Family Values’, and ‘Who Framed Roger Rabbit’.

Lloyd also starred in some famous TV shows including ‘Taxi’, ‘Road to Avonlea’, and ‘Amazing Stories’.
He is an icon in the film industry.

Awards & Achievements

The first form of recognition for his work came to Christopher Lloyd in the form of the Obie Award. Later in 1973, Lloyd won the Best Performance Drama Desk Award. He won two consecutive Emmy Awards for his role in the TV show ‘Taxi’ in the year 1982 and 1983.

Then in 1992, Lloyd won yet another Primetime Emmy Award for his role in ‘Road to Avonlea’. In 1994, he won the Best Supporting Male Independent Spirit Award for his role in ‘Twenty Bucks’.

In 2017, he was recently awarded the National Academy of Video Game Trade Reviewers Award for lead performance in a comedy.
