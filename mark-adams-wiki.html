 Professional career: Mark Adams moved to Texas Tech after playing basketball at South Plains College and serving as a student assistant under head coach Gerald Myers. Adams got his first collegiate coaching job after graduating from Texas Tech in 1979, at Clarendon College, where he lasted for two seasons and won 46 games. He was the head coach at Wayland Baptist from 1983 to 1987, when he went 100–39 overall and guided the Pioneers to the 1985 NAIA title game. Adams went on to become the head coach at West Texas A&M University, where he led the Buffaloes to two NCAA Division II tournament berths with a 108–40 overall record.
