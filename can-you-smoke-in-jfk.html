Can you smoke in JFK?

Yes, you can smoke in designated areas at John F. Kennedy International Airport (JFK). While smoking is prohibited in most indoor public places in New York City, including the airport terminals, JFK provides a few designated smoking areas for passengers who wish to smoke. These designated smoking areas are equipped with proper ventilation systems to ensure that the smoke does not affect non-smoking passengers or airport employees.

Is smoking allowed inside the airport terminals?

No, smoking is not allowed inside the airport terminals at JFK. Smoking is strictly prohibited in all indoor public areas, including the terminals, as per New York City’s Clean Indoor Air Act. This act prohibits smoking in any indoor area accessible to the public, including airports, restaurants, bars, and workplaces.

Where can I smoke at JFK?

JFK has designated smoking areas located outside the terminal buildings. These areas are typically situated near the entrances or exits of the terminals, ensuring easy access for passengers who need to smoke. These designated smoking areas provide a suitable environment for smoking while preventing the smoke from entering the terminal buildings.

Are the designated smoking areas easily accessible?

Yes, the designated smoking areas at JFK are easily accessible. They are located near the entrances or exits of the terminal buildings, ensuring convenient access for passengers who wish to smoke. The airport staff can also provide directions to the nearest smoking area if needed. It’s important to note that smoking is only allowed in these designated areas and not permitted anywhere else inside the airport premises.

Is smoking allowed before going through security?

Yes, smoking is allowed before going through the security checkpoints at JFK. However, once you have cleared security and entered the terminal, smoking is strictly prohibited. If you need to smoke after clearing security, you will need to exit the terminal and use one of the designated smoking areas located outside the terminal buildings.
