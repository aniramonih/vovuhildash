Early Life & Biography

Larry Elder was born in the suburbs of Los Angeles city and grew up in a black neighborhood in Pico-Union of South Central California. His schooling was completed at Washington Preparatory High School and Crenshaw High School. Later he pursued a degree in political science from Brown University and graduated in 1974. He also completed J.D. from the University of Michigan Law School in 1977.

Personal Life

Larry was born in L.A. to Randolph and Viola Elder. His father joined the US Marines after Pearl Harbour was attacked in World War II. Randolph Elder was stationed with the Montford Point Marines. The U.S. Government posthumously awarded him the Congressional Medal of Honour. His brother Kirk Elder has been Larry’s support since the start of his career. He has helped Larry to write books, pursue politics, and even in his career as an attorney. Kirk died just 16 days before his 70th birthday.

Career

He shifted to Ohio after college and worked at a law firm in Cleveland to practice litigation. In 1980, he founded his law firm named “Laurence A Elder and Associates.” It was around at this time that he started his T.V. show based on everyday issues. In the 1990s, it was renamed to “The Larry Elder Show.”

He did a lot of shows right up to the 2010s and won a bunch of different awards for his contribution to create awareness among the American people. He is also a columnist for various newspaper publications across America. For his numerous T.V. contributions, he was given his most significant recognition when he got a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame. Now, he makes YouTube videos for The Epoch Times, having 185k subscribers, which has run into controversies for being pro-Trump.

He was a libertarian but then registered for the Republican Party and became pro-Trump. He also once considered running for the United States Senate against Barbara Boxer in 2010.
