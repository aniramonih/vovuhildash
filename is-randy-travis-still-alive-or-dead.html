Is Randy Travis Still Alive or Dead?

Randy Travis is an American country and gospel artist and songwriter. Randy’s career spanned decades from 1978 to 2013.

Randy Travis was born Randy Bruce Traywick on May 4, 1959. He began singing in church at the age of 8 and his father encouraged him to pursue music. In 1978, Travis recorded his first single Dreamin, the song was a flop. He released She’s My Woman same year, the song was on Billboard 100 for six weeks. He released his second album Always & Forever in 1987, it did very well. Four singles on the album topped the charts. The album went platinum, selling 3 million copies.

Travis was a part of “Voices that Cares” to raise funds for the troops during the gulf war. In 1998, Travis won a Grammy for Same Old Train. By 2000, Randy sang more gospel songs like Baptism and Everywhere We Go.

Is Randy Travis Still Alive or Dead?

In 2013, Randy Travis suffered a massive stroke, he had to undergo surgery. He recovered but had to go through years of rehabilitation and therapy to walk and sing again. By 2020, he made a full recovery and released a single Fools Love Affair. Travis is still very much alive despite years of therapy.
