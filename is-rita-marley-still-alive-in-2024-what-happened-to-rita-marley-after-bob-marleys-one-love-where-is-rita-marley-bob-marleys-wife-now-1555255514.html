Who is Rita Marley?

Rita Marley, widely known as the wife of the iconic reggae musician Bob Marley, is a prominent figure in her own right. Born Alfarita Constantia Anderson in Cuba in 1946, she later moved to Jamaica at a young age.

Raised in the vibrant neighborhood of Trenchtown in Kingston, Jamaica, Rita's upbringing laid the foundation for her future endeavors in music and philanthropy. She became an integral part of Bob Marley's life, supporting him throughout his musical career and beyond.

Apart from her role as Bob Marley's wife, Rita is a talented singer, songwriter, and entrepreneur. Alongside Marcia Griffiths and Judy Mowatt, she formed the vocal group the I Threes, providing backup vocals for Bob Marley and the Wailers.
