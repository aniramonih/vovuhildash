Thurman Munson Net Worth, Income, Salary, Earnings, Biography

Thurman Lee Munson (June 7, 1947 – August 2, 1979) was a Major League Baseball catcher in the United States. He spent his whole 11-year professional baseball career (1969–1979) with the New York Yankees. Munson is the first Yankee to win the Rookie of the Year and Most Valuable Player honours in the same season. Munson, who was born in Akron, Ohio, was the fourth overall selection in the 1968 Major League Baseball Draft. Munson batted over.300 in his two Minor League seasons, solidifying his status as a strong prospect. Late in the 1969 season, he was named the starting catcher for the New York Yankees. Munson’s first full season came in 1970, when he was named American League Rookie of the Year after hitting.302. Munson, the Yankees’ “heart and soul,” was chosen the team’s first captain since Lou Gehrig. From 1976 through 1978, he led the Yankees to three straight World Series berths and two consecutive World Series victories in 1977 and 1978. Munson died at the age of 32 in 1979 at Akron-Canton Airport while practising landing his Cessna Citation. Munson died of asphyxiation as a consequence of a fractured neck sustained in the accident. His two friends managed to flee the burning plane.
